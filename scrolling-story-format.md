# Scrolling Story Format for The Curious Woods

**Date:** November 6, 2025

## Inspiration Sources

- **Nature Magazine** photo essays - immersive, scroll-triggered reveals, high-quality imagery
- **Apple product pages** - emotional hook → feature reveal → technical detail → CTA

## Our Educational Adaptation

### Key Differences from Nature's Approach

- **Simpler visuals** - Diagrams and animations, not photography
- **More interaction** - Invites participation, not just passive scrolling  
- **Educational arc** - Build curiosity → Explore → Understand → Practice

### Structure Pattern

**[Hero Section - Full viewport]**
- Visual: Animated geometric pattern or interactive element
- Text: Intriguing question or paradox


**[Section 1]**
- Visual: Simple diagram or animation
- Text: "Have you ever noticed..."
- Interactive element: Click to explore

**[Section 2]**
- Visual transforms based on scroll position
- Text: Building the concept
- Call-to-action: "Try this yourself"

**[Section 3]**
- The reveal - concept clicks into place
- Launch button: "Open the Interactive"

## Example: Continued Fractions Story

1. **Hero**: Ancient Greeks measuring the universe with ratios
2. **Scroll**: "But some numbers refused to be captured..."
3. **Reveal**: Visual of a rectangle subdividing itself
4. **Interactive preview**: Watch it happen once
5. **Launch**: "Now you try - click any point"

## Tone & Philosophy

The celebrations/reveals are like finding secrets in a video game - congratulatory, not evaluative. No "you got it right!" Just: "Look at the structure of this!"

Not gamification - it's revelation of mathematical beauty.

## Technical Considerations

- Scroll-triggered animations (CSS + JS intersection observers)
- Full viewport sections
- Clean, minimal design inspired by Gaudí's stained glass (for math content)
- Focus on the mathematics, not decoration
- Mobile-first responsive design
