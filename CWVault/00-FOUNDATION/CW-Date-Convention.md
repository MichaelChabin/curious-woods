# CW Date Convention
*A formal standard for all Curious Woods content and tools*

**Established:** March 19, 2026 — Story Developer session
**Status:** Authoritative. Apply consistently across Designer, Coder, and Story Developer work.

---

## The Convention

**Year 0 = 10,000 BCE.** Today = approximately 12,026.

All of human history runs in positive numbers. Distances between events are visible at a glance. No subtraction required. A child can feel that 2,000 years separates two events without converting from BC to AD.

---

## How Dates Are Written

### For CE dates (Year 10,001 onward in CW years)

The conventional year is **bolded** within the CW year. The leading `1` is unbolded — it is quiet context, not the number the child reads first.

| CW year | Renders as | Child reads |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 11440 | 1**1440** | 1440 |
| 11599 | 1**1599** | 1599 |
| 12000 | 1**2000** | 2000 |

In the Scale Beam Tool and other interactive displays, the leading `1` is rendered in a smaller font size (suggest 70% of the main numeral size) to reinforce this visual hierarchy.

### For BCE dates (Year 0–10,000 in CW years)

The full CW year is **bold** — there is no conventional counterpart a child would recognize. The BCE equivalent appears in small italics at the end of the entry, available for the curious but not competing for attention.

| CW year | Renders as | Entry format |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 6800 | **6800** | **6800** — Writing invented in Sumer *(3,200 BCE)* |
| 1000 | **1000** | **1000** — First permanent villages *(9,000 BCE)* |

---

## Why This Convention

Standard BC/BCE notation forces subtraction to understand distance. A child told that Rome fell in 476 CE and Julius Caesar was born in 100 BCE must subtract across a zero to find the gap. In CW years, Caesar is 9,900 and Rome's fall is 10,476 — the distance is immediate.

The convention also makes deep time legible. The end of the ice age is Year 0. Agriculture is Year 1,000. Writing is Year 6,800. The entire arc of civilization fits on a positive number line that a child can walk.

---

## Application

- **Reference Atlas** (`00-FOUNDATION/reference-atlas/`) — all timelines use CW years
- **Scale Beam Tool** — timeline mode uses CW years; leading `1` rendered smaller
- **Story pages** — dates in story text use CW years with BCE gloss where helpful
- **Montparnasse and future stories** — apply convention when dates appear

---

## What This Convention Does Not Cover

- Deep time (before 10,000 BCE) — a separate convention may be needed; Year 0 at 100,000 BCE is one option (today = 112,026). Defer until a deep-time story requires it.
- Future dates — not currently needed.
