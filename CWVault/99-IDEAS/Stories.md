- **Euclidean space on small scales (Apr 23, 2026).** Story seed: invite the child to explore space. Her room is cluttered; ask her to imagine empty space. Place 0 and 1 anywhere, one meter apart. Draw the line between them. Use circles (Euclidean construction) to find the integers — positive in both directions, like a map. Then imagine the Earth as a perfectly smooth sphere beneath her. The line appears to be on the surface. But after ~100 meters the gap between the line (now a tangent) and the spherical surface is enough to slide a piece of paper into. This is what Gauss and Einstein meant by "on a small enough scale, all space is Euclidean" — made concrete, measurable, and quantified. Serves #1 (geometry/numbers), #10 (modeling), #11 (measure by counting), #14 (civilizations think differently — the leap from Euclidean to non-Euclidean is a worldview shift). Would live naturally on Glass with zoom + pixel-counting tools.
