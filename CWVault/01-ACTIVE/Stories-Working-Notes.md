## Stories discussed in this chat

All are candidates for the seed corpus. None are drafted yet.

### The Line constellation

Stories about using a line to probe physical space. Each is standalone but they share a sensibility and a recurring move: *here is a simplification you've been using without noticing; here is the scale at which it breaks.*

- **Zero and One** — child places 0 and 1; the line appears; circles step off integers. Seeds #1, #6.
- **The Ground Curves** — extend the line along the Earth; the gap between line and sphere follows g ≈ d²/(2R); 1 mm at 113 m, 1 m at 3.6 km. First instance of the "how local is local?" move that recurs across the constellation. Seeds #5, #10, #11.
- **Nadia's Bronze Bar** — friend walks away banging bronze bars; the bang arrives late; the child measures the speed of sound with her legs. Seeds #5, #9, #10.
- **Nadia's Flashlight** — the same experiment with light; light is too fast for legs; she reasons instead of measures. Seeds #5, #10, #17.
- **The Line of Lights** — two lights synchronized, one sent out; they drift out of sync; the line is still straight but the news is old. *Beat 1 of what was originally a single "whip" episode.* Seeds #11, #17.
- **The Whip** — anchor the line to the spinning Earth; the image of the line curves because different parts of it are showing light of different ages. *Beat 2.* Seeds #5, #10, #17.
- **How Thin?** — walk down through scales; at some point "line" stops meaning what you thought. Seeds #5, #7, #17.

### The Circle constellation (sketched only)

- **Staying the Same Distance** — the locus idea; rope and stake. Seeds #1.
- **Counting to π / The Wrapping** — pixel-counting story; π emerges by refinement. The prototypical screen-as-ruler story. Seeds #1, #8, #9, #11.
- **Coming Back** — periodicity; pendulum, day, year. Seeds #2, #8.
- **Everyone Looks the Same** — rotational symmetry; why round things are round.
- **Inside and Outside** — a circle splits the plane; a sphere splits space. Containment, flux.
- **Circles from Motion** — spin a line about its endpoint; the circle is the record of a rotation. Bridges to *The Whip*.
- **Circles All the Way Down** — companion to *How Thin?*; at what scale does "circle" stop meaning circle?

## Structural decisions made in this chat

Listed because they took time to reach and shouldn't have to be reconstructed:

1. **Stories are atomic, not sequenced.** Each is a whole thing. No arc can be enforced. The dashboard is a library of siblings. Maya chooses what to surface; the child chooses what to open.

2. **Stories rhyme across the dashboard.** Atomicity does not mean isolation. Maya notices kinships quietly. Multiple stories seeding the same intuition is *correct*, not redundant — stories are sediment, not steps.

3. **Three categories of story: Probe, Anchor, Tale.** Probe stories serve named cognitive intuitions. Anchor stories create time/place/process markers. Tales are stories as themselves, which do cultural-fluency work *because* they are not trying to. All three are load-bearing.

4. **Maya is the narrator of stories.** Warm second-person, aware of the child's history and chosen friend-icons. Not a tutor. Infinitely patient.

5. **Friend-icons.** Maya will eventually ask the child to name friends (real or invented). Those names populate stories consistently — a friend used in a science story is also known in a Florence story, with continuity.

6. **Glass is geometry; there will be other venues.** Glass is the geometry/space venue. Simulation is the natural second venue. Each venue has a small grammar and can carry many overlays. Michael's phrase: "we don't need hundreds of interactives; we need venues with overlays."

7. **The screen is a ruler.** Because the platform runs on a computer, we can count pixels. Books can't. This makes calculus-class moves (integration, differentiation, limits) available to young children by direct counting, without the symbolic apparatus. Named as Intuition #11.

8. **Seed corpus ≠ full library.** Within a year or so, the model running Maya will likely be capable of writing stories in the platform's voice, adjusting them for specific children, generating new ones. Our job now is to write *enough to demonstrate the sensibility* — probably 20–30 stories across categories — not to cover every topic. The seed corpus is example-for-extrapolation.

9. **Practice means effortless returning, not drill.** The Zen sense. The platform's forms of practice are built to resemble how children already learn, not how schools teach. Choice, scheduling, method matching material. Short sessions. No punishment. No gating.

10. **The postcard / share feature.** Every story or piece of work the child makes can be sent to named recipients — grandmothers, best friends, aunts. The child enters the emails. The work goes from her to the named person only; not to servers, not to training data, not to public galleries. *The work is real because real people read it.*
